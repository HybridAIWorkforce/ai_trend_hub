## Dashboard Overview

The main dashboard shows:
- **Total trend items** across all sources.
- **Category breakdown** — General AI, Sales AI, Recruitment AI, Reddit.

## Creating an Article

### Step 1: Select a Trend
From the dashboard, click any trend item to view details, then click "Write Article" to start the wizard.

Alternatively, go to **Articles → Create New Article**.

### Step 2: Configure Article Settings
- **Audience** — Who you're writing for (default: small business owners).
- **Goal** — Educate, thought leadership, how-to, or news analysis.
- **Tone** — Conversational, professional, bold, or analytical.
- **Format** — Short blog (600-800 words), standard (1000-1500), deep dive (2000+).
- **AIDA Framework** — Optional toggle for engagement-optimized structure.

### Step 3: Choose an Angle
The AI generates 3-5 unique article angles. Pick the one that resonates.

### Step 4: Review Outline
An outline is generated with introduction, body sections, and conclusion. You can modify it before proceeding.

### Step 5: Generate Draft
The article streams in real-time, written in your voice using your IP frameworks.

### Step 6: Edit & Polish
Use the Article Editor to refine:
- Edit title and content directly.
- Save versions for comparison.
- Export as Markdown or HTML.
- Generate social media posts.

## Social Media Post Generation

### Generating Posts
1. Open an article in the editor.
2. Click **"Generate Social Posts"** in the Actions panel.
3. Select which platforms to generate for (Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Facebook).
4. Posts appear with character counts and platform-specific formatting.

### Editing Posts
- **Click any post** to enter edit mode.
- Character count updates live as you type.
- Green = safe, Yellow = approaching limit, Red = over limit.

### Hashtag Management
- **Active hashtags** appear below each post.
- **Alternative hashtags** can be swapped in with one click.
- Hashtags are topic-aware, based on article content analysis.

### Copy & Use
Click the **Copy** button to copy any post to your clipboard, ready to paste into the platform.

## CTA Management

### Setting Up CTAs
Go to **Settings → Call-to-Action Settings**:

1. **Enable CTAs** — Master toggle to include CTAs in articles.
2. **Book Promotion** — Add your book title, description, and buy link.
3. **Video/Course** — Add video title and link.
4. **Affiliate Products** — Add product recommendations with affiliate links.
5. **Custom CTAs** — Add any other calls-to-action (consulting, newsletter, etc.).

### CTA Style
- **Invitational** (recommended) — Friendly, not pushy.
- **Direct** — Clear action language.
- **Educational** — Frame as learning opportunity.

### CTA Placement
- **End of article** — CTAs appear at the conclusion.
- **Middle** — CTAs appear mid-article.
- **Both** — CTAs at both points.

### CTA Analytics
Click **"View Analytics"** in the CTA Performance section to see:
- Impressions, clicks, and copies per CTA type.
- Recent activity feed.
- 30-day performance summary.

### Active CTA Preview
At the bottom of the CTA section, see a preview of all currently active CTAs with validation status (green check = valid, red alert = invalid URL).

## Tips & Best Practices

1. **Let the AI draft, then refine** — The first draft captures your voice; polish for precision.
2. **Use AIDA sparingly** — Best for thought leadership and how-to articles. Skip for news analysis.
3. **Rotate social posts** — Generate multiple times to get variety, then pick the best.
4. **Keep CTAs authentic** — The invitational style performs best for building trust.
5. **Check character counts** — Twitter's 280-character limit is strict; edit down if needed.
6. **Cross-industry content** — Articles touching multiple industries get broader reach.
7. **Save versions** — Use "Save as New Version" before major edits so you can compare.
